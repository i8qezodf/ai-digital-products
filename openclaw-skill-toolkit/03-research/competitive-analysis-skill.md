# Competitive Analysis Skill

Research competitors systematically to inform your strategy.

## When to Use

Starting a new product, reassessing market position, or planning to expand. Use when you need to understand the competitive landscape.

## The Skill

```
Analyze the competitive landscape for:

MARKET/PRODUCT: [what you're competing in]
YOUR PRODUCT: [what you offer, briefly]
DEPTH: [quick scan / deep dive]

COMPETITORS TO ANALYZE:
[Name, URL — or "find top 5 in this market"]

ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK:

1. MARKET OVERVIEW
   - Market size and growth
   - Key trends
   - Barriers to entry
   - Regulatory factors

2. COMPETITOR PROFILES
   For each competitor:
   - What they do: [one sentence]
   - Target customer: [who they serve]
   - Pricing model: [and typical range]
   - Strengths: [what they do well]
   - Weaknesses: [where they struggle]
   - Market position: [premium/budget/niche/broad]
   - Customer sentiment: [what users love/hate]

3. COMPARISON MATRIX
                | You | Comp A | Comp B | Comp C
   -------------+-----+--------+--------+--------
   Price        |     |        |        |
   Features     |     |        |        |
   Ease of use  |     |        |        |
   Support      |     |        |        |
   Market share |     |        |        |

4. MARKET GAPS
   - Underserved customer segments
   - Feature gaps
   - Pricing gaps
   - Positioning opportunities

5. STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS
   - Where to compete directly
   - Where to differentiate
   - Threats to watch for
   - Opportunities to pursue

6. SOURCES
   [List sources used]
```

## Example

```
MARKET: Note-taking apps
YOUR PRODUCT: Minimalist, AI-powered notes for developers
DEPTH: Deep dive

COMPETITORS: Notion, Obsidian, Roam Research, Bear

ANALYSIS:

MARKET OVERVIEW:
- $3.2B market, 15% YoY growth
- Remote work driving adoption
- AI integration is the differentiator wave

COMPETITOR PROFILES:

Notion:
- Target: Teams, enterprises
- Pricing: $8-15/user/mo
- Strengths: Flexibility, templates, integrations
- Weaknesses: Slow, complex, subscription fatigue
- Position: Swiss army knife

Obsidian:
- Target: Power users, knowledge workers
- Pricing: Free (local), $25 sync, $50 publish
- Strengths: Local-first, linking, privacy
- Weaknesses: Learning curve, no mobile app
- Position: Tool for serious note-takers

MARKET GAPS:
- Developers specifically (most tools are general)
- Clean, fast mobile experience
- AI that understands code context
- Affordable for individuals

STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS:
- Don't compete on features with Notion
- Position as "Obsidian but easier and with AI"
- Target individual developers, not teams
- Pricing: $5-8/mo individual, not per-seat
```

## Tips

- Look at negative reviews of competitors — that's where opportunities live
- Don't just catalog features — understand why they made choices
- Think about what you'd do differently if you were starting fresh
- Update this quarterly — competitive landscape changes fast

## Variations

**Feature comparison:** Deep dive on specific capabilities
**Pricing analysis:** Research and compare pricing models
**Win/loss analysis:** Interview customers who chose competitors
